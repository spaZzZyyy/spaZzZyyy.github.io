What is Nigel

Nigel is a 2D platformer, Nigel is a mouse with the ability to conduct to change the tempo of the music affecting the tempo of the platforms of his surrounding environment. Nigel also has the ability to enter MAD mode changing his environment to switch the state of the platforms in the environment.

Worked in a team of 4

Nigel was developed in a team of 4! In our teamwork we were able to problem solve multiple issues that arose together, maintaining a modular work flow, communicating needs and ideas, as wells as solving merge conflicts through our github version control.

My Contribution

In our team I took lead of designing and coding a lot of the core mechanics, such as our music manager, movement, and health manager. After implementing theses systems through iterations I communicated how they functioned so my team could use and build upon.

I also took charge of our visuals. Including our animations, shaders, post processing effects, lights, particle systems. Nigel was designed in Unity and used the universal render pipeline.

❮ ❯

Shaders

Shaders were designed using Unity shader graphs. Shaders dictate what game objects look like, using shaders I was able to achieve a lot of visual effects.

❮ ❯

Rigs and Animation

I designed and implemented all of the animation rigs starting from scratch, from concept art I designed to moving sprites which helped game feel. Nigel's tail is done using procedural animation, and rigs were created in using photoshop and Unity skin editor to create bones and weight paint. With sprites designed using Adobe Fresco. I was then able to use these rigs to quickly iterate animations and implement into the game.
